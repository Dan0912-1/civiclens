CapitolKey · Civic Color Variants

1 · Bipartisan

Plum/purple is literally red + blue mixed — the most overtly nonpartisan move possible. The hero headline runs a red-purple-navy gradient. Crimson and navy show up as House/Senate chamber chips on each card, but the dominant brand color is the bipartisan blend.

Meaning
Purple = nonpartisan
Red/blue used as
Chamber indicators
Palette

2 · Stars & Stripes

The flag, but tasteful. Cream parchment base, navy ink headlines, equal-weight crimson and Capitol gold accents, and a subtle red-cream-navy stripe across the top of the hero. Most patriotic, but balanced — both party colors carry equal visual weight.

Meaning
American civic / flag-coded
Risk
Could read as patriotic kitsch
Palette

3 · Chambers

Red and blue aren't decoration — they have a job. Crimson means House, navy means Senate, and they show up as the left stripe and chamber pill on every bill card. Topic colors stay soft pastels so the chamber stripe is always the loudest signal. Functional + meaningful.

Meaning
Red = House, Blue = Senate
Strength
Color teaches the user something
Palette

4 · Constitution

Aged parchment yellow, ink navy headlines, seal red CTA, Capitol gold accents. Reads as "founding documents" — historical and weighty rather than playful. Heaviest of the four. Italic Instrument Serif throughout gives it a hand-engraved feel.

Meaning
Founding documents / civic history
Mood
Weighty, slow, authoritative
Palette
